Welcome

Welcome to the Battle of the Skies project website. Here, you'll soon be able to explore the history of weather modification attempts in the Alps. The site will feature a rich collection of geospatial evidence, including digitized maps, archival data, and advanced remote sensing and satellite information.

This website is a collaborative effort. Luca Scholz, who is writing a book on this topic, will complete the site later this year. You can reach him at luca.scholz@manchester.ac.uk. My colleague, Aadityaa Mehrotra, and I have built this website as an initial framework for Luca to work from. While the site currently serves as a foundation, it will be enriched with detailed content soon.

For now, take a look at the features of the website and explore the preliminary resources we've gathered. Stay tuned for exciting updates as we continue to develop this project.

Title 2

Visualizing Historical Cannons

The cannons can be visualized as they were in the 1800s in red (displayed by default) as well as in different years, and in different colours.

Title 3

Eumetsat Severe Weather Video Overlay 1

If you click the subtitle above, you can see that videos of severe weather events can be overlaid on the map. This may take a few seconds to load.

Title 4

Eumetsat Severe Weather Video Overlay 2

We found 3 Videos of severe weather hail storms in 2023 that can be overlaid on the map, that were taken from the Eumetsat satellites.

Title 5

Eumetsat Severe Weather Video Overlay 3

On the bottom left of the map, there are also other widgets that show live weather data from eumetsat that can be overlayed by the user.

Title 6

Spotlight on Key Location

We have also developed a a few different spotlight features that allows you to focus on a key location on the map. please click the subtitle above to see the spotlight feature in action.
